RS-25 Rocket Engine

The RS-25, also known as the Space Shuttle Main Engine (SSME), is a high-performance, reusable liquid-fuel cryogenic rocket engine developed by NASA. Originally used on the Space Shuttle, it continues to power NASA's Space Launch System (SLS) for deep space missions such as Artemis.

Technical Overview

  • Propellants: Liquid Oxygen (LOX) and Liquid Hydrogen (LH2)
  • Mixture Ratio (O/F): 6.03:1
  • Cycle Type: Fuel-rich dual-shaft staged combustion
  • Vacuum Thrust: 2,279 kN (512,300 lbf)
  • Chamber Pressure: 20.64 MPa (2,994 psi)
  • Specific Impulse (Vacuum): 452.3 seconds (4.436 km/s)
  • Thrust-to-Weight Ratio: 73.1
  • Nozzle Expansion Ratio: 78
  • Combustion Chamber Count: Single
  • Engine Status: Reusable
  • Throttle Range: 67% to 109% of its rated power level
  • Cooling Type: Regenerative cooling
  • Cooling Description: Supercooled liquid hydrogen (LH2) is circulated through cooling channels around the combustion chamber and nozzle to absorb heat before being injected into the combustion zone, improving efficiency and preventing thermal damage.
